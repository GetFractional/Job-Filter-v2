# Editorial Credibility

## Goal

Make Job Filter feel like a premium, truthful, Profile-led workspace that helps users target roles they can credibly win.

## Principles

- Warm-neutral, not cold slate SaaS
- Premium, not flashy
- Calm confidence, not “AI magic”
- Document workflow, not dashboard clutter
- Richer tinted surfaces, not flat white-on-white stacks
- Contained layouts, not board spill or accidental horizontal drift

## Visual Rules

- Use forest-led primary accents and warm neutrals
- Use copper and muted blue carefully for emphasis or review states
- Use serif only for selective editorial moments
- Keep controls and dense content in sans-serif
- Prefer large spacing and strong sectioning
- Make the preview feel like a real artifact on paper
- Keep public CTAs more assertive than internal progress CTAs
- Keep internal workspaces calm and slightly quieter than public marketing surfaces

## Anti-Patterns

- Purple-forward startup look
- Default dashboard card stacks
- Overdecorated gradients
- Cheap gradient badges or statement boxes
- Flat white card soup with weak hierarchy
- Freeform design-tool affordances
- Hiding work behind black-box loaders

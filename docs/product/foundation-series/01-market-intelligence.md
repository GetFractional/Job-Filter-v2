## JTBD

### Core Job

When an experienced applicant is trying to turn a broad career history into a focused job-search system, they need a trustworthy way to choose the right roles, define the right story, and reuse that work across applications without losing credibility.

### Functional Jobs

1. Decide whether a role is worth real effort.
2. Define which lane and story the role belongs to.
3. Extract and organize the most relevant experience before drafting.
4. Produce assets and outreach from approved context.
5. Review what was used, missing, or excluded before sending.
6. Track application progress and learn what works by lane, asset, and company profile.

### Emotional and Social Jobs

- Feel less scattered and less reactive in a noisy market.
- Preserve professional credibility while using software assistance.
- Avoid the shame of sending generic, inflated, or weak-fit applications.
- Feel that setup work compounds instead of disappearing into one-off documents.

## Binding Requirements for Downstream Work

The following requirements are now binding unless a later governance reset overrides them:

1. `Free tier` and free-to-start clarity are trust requirements, not only pricing decisions.
2. No current-state promise may imply or suggest auto-apply.
3. Guided story extraction is the preferred framing for how Profile turns broad experience into usable inputs.
4. Multiple role lanes must remain a first-class user need across brand, website, and app flows.
5. Application tracking and later A/B asset testing are downstream needs in the product story, not optional nice-to-haves.
6. Review-before-send and inspectable reasoning are trust architecture, not polish.

## Not Yet Proven

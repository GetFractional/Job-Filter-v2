## Category Framing

### Primary Category

Job Filter is a truth-first job-search workspace for serious operators.

### Working Category Explanation

It is a Profile-led system for experienced applicants who need to target fewer, better-fit roles, extract the right story from broad experience, and review what belongs before they apply.

### Category Boundaries

Job Filter is not:

- a generic AI copilot
- a resume optimization utility
- a generic job tracker
- an auto-apply engine

### Why This Category Wins

- It matches the market’s trust problem, not just the writing problem.
- It keeps the system anchored in deliberate search quality instead of application volume.
- It leaves room for tracking, learning, and premium outputs without promising more than the product can support today.

## Brand Promise

Build one job profile, use it to drive better-fit applications, and review what belongs before you send anything out.

### Promise Usage

- Use as the clearest top-level promise in strategy docs and high-trust product storytelling.
- Keep the whole sentence intact when trust and mechanism need to travel together.
- Do not compress it into vague speed language.

## Positioning Statement

For experienced applicants managing multiple credible role lanes, Job Filter is a Profile-led workspace that helps them target fewer, better-fit roles, extract the right story from their experience, and review what was used, missing, or excluded before anything carries forward.

Unlike fragmented manual stacks, generic AI tools, or auto-apply products, it keeps targeting, story extraction, drafting, and reasoning connected in one inspectable system.

### Positioning Usage

- Use in internal strategy, pitch framing, product planning, and page-set alignment.
- Do not paste this whole block directly into hero copy.

## Value Proposition

Job Filter helps experienced applicants turn scattered experience into a reusable Profile that improves role selection, strengthens applications, and keeps final outputs closer to approved truth.

### Value Proposition Usage

- Use in hero support copy, overview paragraphs, onboarding framing, and later lifecycle summaries.
- Keep it simpler than the positioning statement.

## Core Message Architecture

The brand should move in this sequence.

| Layer | Job | Locked message | What belief it should create |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 01. Strategic outcome | Show the better destination | Target fewer roles that you can credibly win. | A lower-volume search can be smarter, not smaller |
| 02. Core mechanism | Make the system tangible | Build one job profile that carries the right story forward. | Setup becomes reusable leverage |
| 03. Change work | Reframe what the product is actually doing | Profile helps extract, organize, and reuse the right story before each application. | The product is doing thinking support, not generic text generation |
| 04. Operational payoff | Show how the work compounds | Use Profile to shape resumes, cover letters, outreach, and targeting without starting over each time. | The work connects across the search instead of fragmenting |
| 05. Trust layer | Reduce credibility fear | Review what was used, missing, or excluded before you accept a draft. | The user keeps control and can verify the reasoning |
| 06. Entry assurance | Lower commitment friction | Start free, understand the workflow, then decide whether deeper leverage is worth paying for later. | The first step feels safe and concrete |

## Differentiators

| Differentiator | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| One reusable Profile | The user stops rebuilding the same story from scratch for every role |
| Guided story extraction before formatting | Broad, nonlinear experience gets translated before it is turned into assets |
| Targeting and drafting stay connected | Role choice and story quality stay inside one system instead of splitting across tools |
| Review-before-send logic | Trust is earned through used, missing, and excluded reasoning |
| Search continuity beyond setup | The product story extends into application tracking and later learning, not only draft creation |

## Headline Territories

Use these as the approved brand territories. Each territory has exactly three approved variants.

### Outcome Territory

1. Target fewer roles that you can credibly win.
2. Put more energy into roles that actually fit your story.
3. Narrow your search to roles you can defend with confidence.

Use the first line as the default locked variant.

### Mechanism Territory

1. Build one job profile. Use it to drive better-fit applications.
2. Turn scattered experience into one Profile you can reuse with intent.
3. Build your Profile once, then shape each application from approved truth.

Use the first line as the default locked variant.

### Trust Territory

1. Check what was used, missing, or excluded before you apply.
2. Review the logic before anything carries forward.
3. Keep unsupported claims out of the final draft.

Use the first line as the default locked variant.

## CTA Ladder

These are the approved working controls for this chapter. They should stay stable unless a later packet explicitly reopens them.

| CTA role | Locked control | Use when | Rules |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Public primary | `Build my job profile` | The mechanism is already clear enough that a direct commitment feels believable | Keep a concrete explanation of Profile nearby. Do not place it on vague teaser surfaces. |
| Exploratory | `See how it works` | The user still needs sequence, product proof, or trust explanation | Send it to concrete explanation, not generic browsing. Do not let it outrank the public primary on decisive surfaces. |
| Internal primary | `Continue setup` | The user is already inside Profile and needs progress, not persuasion | Keep it quieter than the public primary. Use it for momentum, not conceptual reselling. |

### CTA Guardrails

- Public CTA language should name a concrete asset or next step.
- Exploratory CTA language should use inspection verbs, not vague inspiration verbs.
- Internal CTA language should describe progress, not persuasion.
- Do not replace the approved ladder with `Start Profile`, `Run AI`, or similar internal shorthand.
- No CTA should imply auto-apply, automatic submission, or recruiter-ready output without user review.

## Trust and Proof Rules

| Rule | Why it matters | Safe example | Risky example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mechanism before outcome | This market needs to understand how the product works before believing the benefit | “Build one job profile, then use it to drive better-fit applications.” | “Get hired faster with AI.” |
| Show the review path | Trust rises when the user can inspect what changed before using it | “Review what was used, missing, or excluded before you accept a draft.” | “We generate recruiter-ready content automatically.” |
| Name the boundary | Constraint language increases credibility | “Suggestions stay visible until you approve them.” | “Every output is ready to send.” |
| Keep free-to-start clarity visible | Pricing ambiguity creates unnecessary suspicion | “Start in the Free tier and see how Profile works before deciding on anything deeper.” | “Upgrade for the real value later.” |
| Preserve no-auto-apply boundaries | The brand must not drift toward risky automation claims | “Job Filter helps you prepare and review before you send.” | “Let the system handle applications for you.” |
| Use proof that exists | Interface evidence and workflow clarity must carry the trust load until stronger proof exists | “The review shows what strengthened the draft and what stayed out.” | “We know exactly what hiring teams want.” |

## Language Classification

### Locked Canonical Language

Use these freely across downstream chapters:

- Target fewer roles that you can credibly win.
- Build one job profile. Use it to drive better-fit applications.
- Check what was used, missing, or excluded before you apply.
- Continue setup.
- Profile helps turn scattered experience into reusable job-search leverage.
- Start free, understand the workflow, then decide whether to go deeper.

### Experimental Acquisition Language

Use only in clearly labeled acquisition testing later. Do not treat these as locked product copy.

- Stop wasting strong experience on weak-fit roles.
- Bring more signal into the applications you actually decide to send.
- Turn a messy work history into a cleaner story before your next application.
- Make it easier to look like the candidate you already are.
- Spend more time on roles you can actually back up.

### Disallowed Proof-Risk Language

Do not use these in locked product or brand copy:

- Get hired faster with AI.
- Land your dream job.
- Beat ATS filters automatically.
- Generate recruiter-ready materials in seconds.
- Let the system apply for you.
- Skip the hard parts of job search.

## Objection Handling in VIE Order

### Vehicle

#### What the audience is asking

Why use this instead of manual editing, spreadsheets, prompts, or generic AI tools?

#### What we should say

Job Filter keeps targeting, story extraction, drafting, and review logic tied to one Profile instead of scattering the work across disconnected tools.

#### What proof supports it

- Profile-first explanation
- guided story extraction framing
- rationale with used, missing, and excluded states
- examples that show targeting and drafting staying connected

### Internal

#### What the audience is asking

Can I actually use this well with my background, my time limits, and my messy experience?

#### What we should say

The first step is not busywork. It creates reusable leverage. Profile helps shape the right story from the experience you already have, and nothing carries forward without review.

#### What proof supports it

- setup framing that shows immediate payoff
- concrete examples of broad backgrounds being translated into lane-specific stories
- clear progress language inside Profile

### External

#### What the audience is asking

Will the market, ATS systems, or employer skepticism make this less useful?

#### What we should say

Job Filter is not promising to fix the market or bypass hiring systems. It helps the user make tighter role decisions, shape more defensible materials, and keep unsupported claims out of the final draft.

#### What proof supports it

- no-auto-apply boundary language
- review-before-send language
- exclusion examples
- clear distinction between suggestion and approved truth
